Mapping physical climate risks — which regions face the greatest threats?

Physical Climate Risks

Climate change creates tangible, location-specific risks to economic assets:

🌊 Sea Level Rise

Coastal flooding threatens property, infrastructure, and insurance markets.

🔥 Extreme Heat

Reduces labor productivity, increases energy demand, damages agriculture.

🌪️ Storms

Hurricanes, wildfires, and droughts cause billions in direct damages.

Building a Composite Risk Score

We combine multiple risk dimensions into a single score per region:

Risk Score = w₁ × Heat + w₂ × Flood + w₃ × Drought + w₄ × Storm + w₅ × Fire

Each component is normalized (0–100 scale) and weighted by economic exposure.

Finding: Coastal and southern US states score highest on composite climate risk, with implications for real estate valuations, insurance pricing, and municipal bond spreads.
