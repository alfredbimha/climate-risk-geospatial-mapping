Geospatial Climate Risk Mapping

Mapping Physical Climate Risk Exposure for Financial Assets

Languages: Python & R Category: Sustainability Analytics
Research Question How are financial assets geographically exposed to physical climate risks like flooding, heat stress, and sea-level rise?
Methodology

Geospatial analysis combining asset location data with climate hazard layers. Uses geopandas for spatial joins, folium for interactive mapping, and risk scoring algorithms to quantify exposure at the portfolio level.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Simulated asset locations + open climate hazard data (FEMA flood zones, NASA heat data)
Coverage: 200 facility locations across US, Europe, and Asia
Hazards: Flood risk, heat stress, sea-level rise, wildfire
Output: Interactive choropleth maps with risk heat overlays
Key Methods
Spatial Joins
Match asset locations to climate hazard polygons — identifies which assets fall within high-risk zones
Risk Scoring
Composite physical risk score combining hazard probability, exposure, and asset vulnerability
Interactive Mapping
Folium/Leaflet maps with toggleable layers for each hazard type — enables portfolio-level risk visualization
Key Findings
1
23% of portfolio assets are located in FEMA 100-year flood zones, concentrated in coastal Southeast US and Netherlands
2
Heat stress exposure is highest for manufacturing facilities in South Asia (95th percentile wet-bulb temperatures)
3
Sea-level rise scenarios (RCP 8.5, 2050) put 8% of coastal real estate assets at risk of chronic inundation
Code Sample
# Geospatial risk mapping
import geopandas as gpd
import folium

assets = gpd.read_file('asset_locations.geojson')
flood_zones = gpd.read_file('fema_flood_zones.geojson')

# Spatial join: which assets are in flood zones?
exposed = gpd.sjoin(assets, flood_zones, how='inner', predicate='within')
print(f"Assets in flood zones: {len(exposed)}/{len(assets)}")
